The Challenge

Design for Mass & Packaging

Design a lighter EV cooling circuit without sacrificing thermal performance — every gram matters on a race car and the chassis leaves almost no room for error.

What I Designed
  • Rerouted tubing and optimized diameter — cut 2 lb from the cooling loop with no thermal penalty
  • Modeled pump welding jig and swirl pot mounting bracket in SolidWorks for tight chassis clearances and weld repeatability
Flow Meter Bench Test Setup Pump · turbine flow meter · radiator · tubing circuit
Test & Validate

Instrument, Measure, Iterate

  • Built bench test circuit with turbine flow meter — compared rated vs. measured pump performance
  • Ran comparative thermal dissipation tests across multiple tubing diameter configurations
  • Documented failure modes and root causes at each iteration; each finding drove the next design revision
Redesigned Swirl Pot Lid — SolidWorks Revised sealing surface · tighter tolerances · SLA printed
Problem Identified

Root Cause → Fix

  • OEM lid was leaking fluid and air under operating pressure
  • Measured the original geometry and traced the failure to a tolerance stack-up on the sealing face — the gap was letting both air and coolant escape
Solution
  • Redesigned the sealing surface with corrected tolerances to close the gap entirely
  • SLA printed instead of FDM — higher resolution surface finish is critical for an airtight coolant seal
Swirl Pot + Radiator — PDM Integration Mockup Positional assembly for master powertrain CAD
Systems Integration

PDM Workflow & CAD Management

  • Built positional mockup of swirl pot and radiator so sub-assemblies could drop directly into the team's master powertrain CAD via SolidWorks PDM
  • Ensures correct spatial relationships across all cooling components before physical build — no clashes at assembly time
